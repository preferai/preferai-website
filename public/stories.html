Analyzing anonymous guest data and predicting guests' interests during holidays

The municipality of Schladming aims to optimize the guest throughput within the organized activities. This can be done by either providing or (temporarily) excluding information about certain activities to guests. For this, particular activities, weather options, locations and category information for defined time frames and optional boosting settings need to be dynamically taken into account.

Outcome:
  • Conducted a thorough analysis and infered guest profiles from anonymized Schladming-Dachstein Sommercard data.
  • Developed a novel context-aware recommendation system which aims to predict a guest's interest in a certain (holiday) activity.

Personalized Culture Events

Every year, the Theaterholding Graz with the Graz Opera, Schauspielhaus, Next Liberty, the Kasematten, the Orpheum or the Dom im Berg registers around 500,000 visitors. To develop and strengthen the connection between customers and cultural event offers of the city of Graz and the province of Styria, expectations and demands of the population have to be takein into consideration when generating insights from the existing data landscape.

Outcome:
  • Conducted a comprehensive data analysis on customer behaviour (e.g., pricing, preferences, etc.).
  • Developed innovative use-cases and design concepts on how to introduce personalization for both anonymous and known customers.

Biomedical Playlist Generator

Older people are often faced with dementia. It has been shown, that people with this illness still respond to music they like. Because of that, the aim of MechaMed is to use a sensor-based system which stimulates the brain by automatically playing songs that cause a positive emotional reaction.

Outcome:
  • Research on algorithms that, using data extracted from wearable devices, are able to uncover the emotional reaction while listening to a song.
  • Developed an automatic playlist generation system, which grounds its decisions in biomedical data.

Brokerage

The Data Market Austria (DMA) offers a platform to bring datasets, data services, consulting, and infrastructure offers to a common marketplace. The goal is to be a central hub for all actors participating in the Austrian data economy, regardless of their industrial and research domain to overcome traditional domain boundaries.

Outcome:
  • Extensive analysis, ideation and conception of the data market landscape.
  • Development of a brokerage system to help users overcome the information overload problem and connect with clients or suppliers in the digital domain.
